# Board Scenario Tool — 2-Minute Demo Script

**Pace:** Brisk, confident, conversational. You're showing Board members a tool, not teaching a class.

---

## OPEN (10 sec)

"This tool lets you test every financial lever available to the gymnastics program — fees, capacity, new revenue, staffing — and see the impact on the budget in real time. Everything is anchored to DPR's own numbers from their March 5 presentation and backed by FOIA documents."

## ORIENT (10 sec)

"Layout is simple. On the left — your scenario levers, organized across five tabs. On the right — what those choices mean for the program. Five KPIs up top, four charts below: revenue, costs, which levers are driving the gains, and cost recovery by program."

*gesture to KPIs, then charts*

"Right now everything is at DPR's baseline. Net tax support: $970K. That's our starting point."

---

## PASS 1 — Quick Touch (50 sec)

*The point: show the tool is responsive and covers everything. One move per tab, don't linger.*

### Cost Recovery (5 sec)
"Tab one — the cost recovery pyramid. This is the policy foundation. Where should gymnastics sit? Right now DPR has it in the lowest tier. Click Moderate —"

*click Moderate preset*

"— and that shifts the recovery targets up across the board."

### Fees & Rates (15 sec)
"Fees tab. Rec classes are $13.80. Private gyms charge $28 to $35. Let's bring this to $18."

*slide rec fee to ~$18*

"Watch the market position chart — we're still well below every private competitor. And the competitive rate — currently around $8 an hour — let's nudge that to $10."

*slide comp rate to $10*

"Look at the fee table — you can see exactly what each level would pay and how it compares to today."

### Capacity (10 sec)
"Capacity tab. We can add sections, fill those three vacant positions that are already budgeted — that's just revenue, no new cost — and bring in GWU athletes to both reduce temp staffing and teach rec classes."

*toggle Fill Vacant, toggle GWU Athletes*

### Revenue Streams (10 sec)
"New programs. Birthday parties, private lessons, open gym, clinics, camps — these are all standard at every private gym in the area. Toggle a few on —"

*toggle birthday, private lessons, open gym*

"— and you see each one stacking in the lever chart on the right."

### Accounting (10 sec)
"And the accounting tab lets you adjust overhead assumptions and management staffing. Drop from 3 FTE to 2 —"

*click 2 FTE radio*

"— that's $85K in savings."

### Check the Scoreboard (5 sec)
*gesture to KPIs*

"Already moved the needle significantly, and we've barely pushed anything hard. Look at Net Tax Support — it's already dropping."

---

## PASS 2 — Full Potential (40 sec)

"Now let me show you what's possible if the county actually runs this like a program that wants to succeed."

*click Full Recovery preset*

"Full cost recovery framework."

*slide rec fee to ~$22, comp rate to $12, NR surcharge to 25%*

"Fees in line with the market — still cheaper than every private option."

*toggle on: Fill Vacant, GWU Athletes, Kim Klingler (enjoy the confetti), Contract Coach*

"Fill every budgeted position. Bring in GWU athletes. Enable contract coaching and direct hire."

*slide comp growth to +30*

"Grow the competitive program back toward its peak — it was 202 athletes in 2020, it's 154 today."

*toggle on remaining new programs — camps, clinics, sponsorships, meets*

"Turn on the revenue streams that every comparable program already offers."

*pause — let the KPIs land*

"Look at the top right. Net Tax **Surplus**. The program isn't just sustainable — it's contributing back. And every single assumption here is grounded in DPR's own data, FOIA documents, and market comps."

---

## CLOSE (10 sec)

"The question isn't whether this program can pay for itself. The data says it can. The question is whether DPR will be given the direction to actually try. This tool lets every Board member see that for themselves."

---

**Total: ~2:00**

*Tips: Don't read this verbatim. Hit the beats, keep your eyes on the tool, let the charts do the talking. The confetti on Kim's toggle is a good laugh line — lean into it.*
